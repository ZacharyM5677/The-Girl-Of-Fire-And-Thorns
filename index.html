Summary


The Girl of Fire and Thorns is about a girl named Lucero-Elisa,
a princess from Orovalle who is chosen by God and carries a
powerful gem called the Godstone in her navel. At the
start of the story, she doesn't feel confident in herself. She thinks
she's too chubby, short, and the opposite of brave. On her 16th birthday,
she’s forced to marry a king named Alejandro from a desert
kingdom called Joya d'Arena. She doesn’t know him, and she’s nervous
due to her insucurities.

As Elisa travels to her new home, their group is attacked by the
Invierne, an enemy nation with dead Godstones which are being
used for their power via special amulets. During the attack, one
of Elisa’s closest companions, Aneaxi, gets badly hurt. Later, Aneaxi dies
because her injury becomes infected, and Elisa feels guilty for not noticing
the injury sooner. This is one of the first moments that shows Elisa starting
to care for others and take responsibility.

Later in the story, Elisa gets taken away by some of her friends, and
finds a village away from the capital of Joya d'Arena. ends up becoming a leader of a rebel group called the Malficio. She helps poor or damaged villages and stands up to
the Invierne. She gets kidnapped by a powerful Invierne sorcerer but tricks
him and escapes by using duerma berries (a sleeping drug) and some
clever thinking after he falls asleep from the berries. She steals a Godstone amulet from the
sorcerer, which helps her later.

Eventually, Elisa loses someone she cares about, Humberto, who might have
loved her. She realizes she doesn’t really love King Alejandro, but still
chose to protect his people. She returns to the capital of Joya d'Arena.
Alejandro almost didn't recognize Elisa, showing how much Elisa has evolved
over the story. As a final attempt, the Invierne invate the capital in attempt
to kill Elisa for her Godstone once in for all. In the final battle, Elisa uses the
Godstone and the amulet she stole to defeat the Invierne army.
Alejandro dies from injuries, and Elisa becomes the Queen of Joya d'Arena.
She has grown into a strong and brave leader.

For my project, I made a social media profile that shows how Elisa
changes throughout the book. In her early posts, I tried to make her sound
insecure about herself, just like she is in the beginning
of the book. As the posts go on, she becomes more confident.
I made sure to add posts about important events, like the caravan
attack, Aneaxi’s death, and her time with the Malficio. I also
showed her relationships with people like Ximena, Humberto, and King Alejandro.
