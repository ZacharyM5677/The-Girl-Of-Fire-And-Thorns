I'm Lucero-Elisa de Riqueza, Princess of Orovalle; Chosen by god,
represented by a blue gem in my navel people call the
"God Stone." Today is my 16th birthday, and have the privallige
of getting married while wearing a suffocating terno. I'm too chubby;
My head is swirrling from my ternos suffocation, and I look so ugly and short.
All I can hope is that my husband is ugly, so when he divorces me,
I feel nothing from how ugly he is. My nurse and guardian, Ximena,
and my lady-in-waiting, Aneaxi, helped me get dressed for the wedding.
Wish me luck!

My husband was so handsome, I quite literally froze in shock when
he pulled up my linen veil to look at my face. He crouched and smiled
at me, and I tried to smile back; My cheeks had failed to obey.
He is Alejandro de Vega of Joya d'Arena.

I was told I had to leave to a desert country named Joya d'Arena
to help fight a war with the country of Invierne.

Today was a disaster, Aneaxi almost died. We were traveling in a caravan to
Joya d'Arena, and everything was going smoothly. I was in a carriage with
Ximena and Aneaxi. Soon however, we heard drums in the distance—drums
where there should've been none; It was the Invierne. They began thrashing
our carriges; including mine and my husbands. Me, Ximena, and Aneaxi
went through the trapdoor on the floor which led us below the carriage
, but I fell on Aneaxi and had broken her leg. Me and Ximena had to
move from the bottom to the side of the carriage and yank Aneaxi out.
Our carriage began to burst into flames somehow, the Invierne weren't
even close. The horses on our carriage were about to move; Ximena and I
began to panic. We yanked Aneaxi hard, but she wouldn't budge. Luckily,
somebody had cut the string connecting the body of the carriage to the
horses. We yanked out Aneaxi, but not before a blood curdling scream
erupted from her chest. Once she was free from the bottom of the carriage,
we ran and ran until we couldn't run anymore. We had almost died.
Once the Invierne retreated, we all returned back to Orovalle;
some injured, some dead. What a nightmare today..

On the trip to Joya d'Arena, Aneaxi died. She didn't want us to feel bad
for her or waste resources on her, so she didn't tell us she had a gaping
scar on the back of her leg. She got it when me and Ximena yanked her
from the bottom of the carriage. Because she decided not to tell us,
it had gotten infected and killed her. I wish she told us sooner.
